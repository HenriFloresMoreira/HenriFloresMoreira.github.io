Changing Perspective

Before be able to change perspective in Eclipse we must first know if the version of eclipse has the Web Perspective
If it does you may skip to Step 6 in Perspective.
If it does not you may follow these steps to get on the right path.

Step 1: To get the Web Perspective we must download it through Eclipse. At the top of Eclipse the Help drop down menu has the option to install new software. This is where we will download it.

Step 2: Within the menu there is a text box at the top and thats were you will enter the a download link that is dependent on your version of Eclipse. The link that you can copy and paste for Mars version: http://download.eclipse.org/releases/mars/ Luna version: http://download.eclipse.org/releases/luna/ and an older version that can be used is http://download.eclipse.org/releases/indigo.

Step 3: After it loads you will see something similar to this image.

Step 4: Scroll through the options to find the Web,XML,Java EE and OSGi Enterprise Development.

Step 5: Click the arrow button to lower the menu for this option and find the four options that are shown in this next image and click on the four and click next. This will download the Web development package.

Step 6: The perspective is normally in Java in Eclipse. To change the perspective you must click on the open perspective button in the top right area.

Step 7: After clicking on the button you will see multiple options and you will click the Web option and the ok button.

You Are Now In Web Perspective
